Product/Service

Surefire Entr. sells custom, user-friendly websites as well as web-hosting services. Businesses who desire to promote their products or services and expand their audience may purchase a website from us at an affordable cost. Clients can simply use the online form on our site to enter all the pages and features they want on their desired website, book a meeting with one of our experienced developers and receive a demo in 3-5 business days.

Target Market

The target market is all businesses, organisations, institutions and entities. Within the next 5 years Surefire plans on opening at least one branch within areas with high business concentration like a town.

Why SureFire

Starting for only $80/month our clients can take advantage of our cloud-based development platform. At each branch clients will experience friendly customer service with free and productive consulting.

Our business falls under the standard business model category. Industry Analysis

The website development industry is currently very promising for new ventures like Surefire. The increased usage in online mediums due to the Covid-19 pandemic has provided a window of opportunity. Many businesses and individuals who were without an online presence, now desire websites to advertise their products or services and Surefire can cater to this need. However, customers typically encounter some problems when trying to purchase website development services online. These issues include the process seeming too difficult/technical and miscommunication between clients and developers leading to websites which are undesirable based on their needs. Surefire avoids these problems by having a simple but effective user interface which allows customers to choose the exact features they want and by providing one-on-one consultations via Zoom so Surefire’s developers can gain a better understanding of the client’s needs. These features put Surefire in a unique position in the industry. At present, it would be difficult for many of Surefire’s competitors, such as Upwork and Fiverr, to replicate the more reliable client-developer interaction due to the fact that outsourcing the development process is at the heart of their business models. These freelancers are not usually vetted by the company and sometimes may “ghost” clients.
